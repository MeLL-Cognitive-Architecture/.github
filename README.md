# MeLL Cognitive Architecture

MeLL Cognitive Architecture is a governed AI architecture designed for industrial and regulated environments where AI must operate with traceability, auditability, and explicit human oversight by design.

It defines a structured architectural model for building AI systems that are not only intelligent, but also controlled, explainable, and operationally reliable.

---

## Definition

MeLL is a cognitive architecture for AI governance, focused on:

- Human-in-the-loop decision control  
- Evidence-based, auditable operations  
- Supervised autonomy  
- Separation of decision and execution  
- Reliability in mission-critical systems  

---

## Core Architectural Model

The architecture is built on:

- Governance by design (policy-driven control systems)  
- DataCore evidence layer (traceability, versioning, and audit artifacts)  
- Multi-agent federated structure  
- Human–AI integration with supervised and conditional autonomy  
- Observability and cognitive security  

All relevant actions follow a controlled flow

---

## Focus Areas

- AI Governance Architecture  
- Industrial AI Systems  
- Human-in-the-loop AI  
- Multi-agent systems  
- Cognitive security and risk-aware AI  
- Hybrid and multicloud environments  

---

## Positioning

MeLL is designed for scenarios where AI must operate under strict control and accountability:

- AI decisions must be auditable  
- Operational risk must be controlled  
- Automation cannot be opaque  
- Human sovereignty must be preserved
